Restructuring the C2 of the GPS, Summer 2017

During my summer at AIS, I was tasked with researching the current Command and Control of the Global Positioning System (GPS). In my research, I was introduced to blockchains. Soon after, my collegue and I wrote a paper to the Vice Chief of Staff of the Air Force on the potential applications of blockchains in the Air Force, honing blockchains'of data validation and virtual ledgers. We were asked to write a follow-up report on the latest Red Belly Blockchain technology and its potential in the Air Force.

Afterwards, I reaccessed the possibility of blockchains in the Command and Control of the GPS. Instead of a typical centralization involving the Master Control System (MCS), my collegue and I created a simulation of a GPS Command and Control that was decentralized. It was a table-top demonstration that included a Javascript Front-End and a Python backend. Blockchains can easily be implemented in our Python backend.

More information can be found in our end-of-summer report.
